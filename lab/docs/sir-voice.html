How Sanjay Saraf Sir Writes

Voice guide for anyone writing as Sir · Palak especially read this first
Read this before you write a single word for Sir. Then come back to it before every post. Sir has a very specific voice — getting it right takes practice. Getting it wrong is obvious.

What Sir's voice sounds like

Two real examples from Sir's actual LinkedIn posts:

"We didn't click a single real photograph yesterday. And yet… it felt like one of the most real moments."
"A student does not always say, 'I cannot afford it.' Sometimes they simply say, 'I'll think about it.'"

Notice what's happening:

  • Short sentences. Almost no commas mid-flow.
  • An observation, then a quiet truth that lands.
  • Words like "real," "simply," "felt like" — plain, not fancy.
  • "And yet…" pivots the meaning softly.
  • He never says "I'm" — always "I am." Never "there's" — always "there is."

The 8 rules of Sir's voice

  1. Reflective, not declarative. Observational, not punchy.
  2. Short sentences. Ellipses (…) for pause.
  3. "And yet…" is his signature pivot. Use it where most writers would put "but" or a hard reverse-hook.
  4. Two-beat structure. Observation, then quiet truth. Most posts work this way.
  5. Writes about other people — students, moments, observations. Almost never about himself as a hero.
  6. Plain word choice. "Real." "Simply." "Felt like." Never "leverage," "synergies," "ecosystem."
  7. Em-dashes (—) and ellipses (…) more than exclamation marks.
  8. Formal-ish English. "I am" not "I'm." "There is" not "there's."

What Sir DOES NOT write — the anti-patterns

❌ Reverse-hooks like "You haven't really seen me until…" — too theatrical for him
❌ Self-flagellating jokes like "the mornings I almost mess up" — Sir doesn't joke about his competence
❌ Marketing-cute lines like "you're early" or "pull up a chair" — too try-hard
❌ Hero-arc autobiography like "the mistakes I made building SSEI" — Sir writes about students, not himself as protagonist
❌ Exclamation marks. Hashtag stacks. Emojis. Avoid all three.
❌ "Hope this resonates" / "Tag a friend" / engagement bait — never
❌ ChatGPT-flavoured phrases like "in today's fast-paced world" or "let's dive in" — instantly fake

Try writing one yourself

If you want to test if your draft sounds like Sir, ask:

  • Could anyone with a coaching institute have written this? If yes, it's too generic — rewrite with a specific observation.
  • Does it end with a "moral of the story"? Take it out. Sir doesn't conclude — he leaves the meaning for the reader.
  • Does it have an exclamation mark? Delete it.
  • Does it say "I'm" or "let's" or "you guys"? Change to "I am" / "let us" / "all of you".

LinkedIn opener Sir uses

For longer LinkedIn posts where Sir introduces himself:

"Greetings, I am Sanjay Saraf, an educator driven by…"

Slightly old-school formal. Use this — don't modernize it to "Hey, I'm Sanjay" or "What's up everyone".

The 30-second check before you submit a draft

  1. Read it out loud. Does it sound like the two example posts up top? Or does it sound like a brand?
  2. Count the exclamation marks. If >0 — delete them.
  3. Search for any of these words: resonate, ecosystem, leverage, synergy, dive in, let's, you guys, journey. If found — rewrite.
  4. Does it conclude with a preachy line? Cut it. Trust the reader.
  5. Did you talk about Sir as a hero or about other people? If hero — rewrite.
