🟦 AKASH — your 4 active tasks for May L2

Task 1: Research 10 finance creators

Week 1 and Week 3

What this means

Find people online who teach finance, talk about CFA/CA exams, or do stock-market content. Write down who they are.

Why we do it

We want these people to tell their audience about SSEI. Before we can reach out, we need to know which ones are worth reaching out to.

How to do it

  1. Open YouTube, Instagram, and LinkedIn.
  2. Search things like: "CFA prep India", "stock market for beginners", "finance career India", "FRM exam prep", "ICAI tips", "investing tutorial".
  3. For each creator you find, ask yourself: Would their audience be interested in SSEI's courses? If yes, log them.
  4. For each one, fill in these columns:
    • Name + handle (like @financewithprashant)
    • Platform (YouTube / IG / LinkedIn)
    • Follower count
    • Their 2 best posts (most views and likes)
    • Who their audience seems to be (college kids? working pros? CFA aspirants?)
    • Can we contact them? (email in bio? open DMs?)

What "done" looks like

10 rows in your sheet, each filled in.

Common mistakes

❌ Picking creators with 50K followers but only 20 likes per post — those followers are fake or dead.
❌ Picking generic finance guys whose audience is 40-year-old traders — they won't buy a CFA course.
✅ Pick a creator with 8K followers who gets 300+ engaged comments from students.

Task 2: Draft and send 5 outreach messages

Week 2

What this means

Pick 5 creators from your research sheet. Write a short message to each. Kavya sends them from her handle.

Why we do it

This is how we start conversations. Most creators won't reply — that's normal. Some will, and those become partnerships.

How to do it

  1. Pick 5 creators you've researched.
  2. For each, write a 40-80 word message with 4 parts:
    • Compliment something specific — mention which post/video, why it stood out (NOT "love your content!")
    • Say who SSEI is in one line
    • Say what you want from them — a mention? Free trial? Collab video?
    • Make the next step concrete — "Open to a 15-min call this Friday?"
  3. Paste into a Google Doc. Share with Kavya.
  4. Kavya sends from her handle.
  5. Track replies in your sheet — column: "Reply? Yes/No/Date".

Example of a good outreach message

Hi Prashant — saw your reel on "What I wish I knew before CFA L1" — the bit about audit ratios was 🔥. We're SSEI, the institute Sanjay Saraf founded — we train ~3,000 CFA/CA students a year. Would love to send you free access to our L1 course and hear what you'd improve. Got 15 min on Friday for a call?

What "done" looks like

5 messages drafted, Kavya has them, you've started tracking replies.

Common mistakes

❌ Generic compliments
❌ Long messages (over 100 words)
❌ Asking for "a quick chat" with no specific purpose
❌ Sending before Kavya reviews

Task 3: Map the buyer journey

Weeks 1, 3, 4 · target: 12 steps by 20 June

What this means

Map the entire path from when someone first hears of SSEI to their first day of class. At each step, write down WHAT message we should send, WHEN, and on WHICH channel. You write the plan — not the actual message.

Why we do it

Right now SSEI's nurture messages are inconsistent. Some students get 5 emails. Some get nothing. We need ONE clean map so the team knows what to send when.

How to do it

  1. Imagine a student named Rahul. He's a B.Com 2nd year. He's searching YouTube for "CFA prep".
  2. Trace his path:
    • Day 0: He finds Sir's reel on Instagram. What should we do? → Save reel, follow account, lead magnet popup
    • Day 1: He clicks our website. → WhatsApp welcome ("Hi Rahul, here's a free CFA L1 readiness quiz")
    • Day 3: Hasn't bought yet. → Email with "5 CFA L1 mistakes" carousel
    • Day 7: Still hasn't bought. → WhatsApp from Kavya with class schedule
    • Day 14: Bought. → Welcome email + class joining link
    • Day 17: First class. → Day-of "looking forward to seeing you" message
  3. For EACH step, fill a row with 5 columns: Stage · Day · Channel · Message theme · Suggested CTA.

Example row

StageDayChannelThemeSuggested CTA
InterestDay 3WhatsAppFree CFA prep quiz"Take the quiz"

What "done" looks like

A Google Sheet with 12+ rows by 20 June.

Common mistakes

❌ Writing the actual message ("Hi Rahul, I noticed you searched for CFA...") — NOT YOUR JOB. Just the theme.
❌ Skipping stages because they feel boring
❌ Only thinking about email — most journeys are multi-channel (IG, WhatsApp, email, phone)

Task 4: Design 10 lead magnet concepts

Week 4

What this means

A "lead magnet" is a free thing we give people in exchange for their email/phone. Examples: free PDF, free quiz, free webinar. You design 10 ideas. The team picks 2-3 and builds them.

Why we do it

SSEI captures very few leads right now. The right lead magnet can 10x our top-of-funnel.

How to do it

For each idea, write a 1-paragraph concept (50-100 words) covering:

  • Title — what's the free thing called?
  • Who it's for — what kind of student?
  • What's inside — what will they actually get?
  • Why they'd want it — the pain point we're solving
  • How to deliver it — PDF? Email? Quiz on the website?

Examples of good lead magnet concepts

"The CFA L1 study planner — 6-month week-by-week roadmap. PDF."
"Am I ready for CFA L1? 10-question quiz that scores your readiness."
"5 mistakes B.Com students make when picking CFA vs CA — short video."

What "done" looks like

10 concepts in a Google Doc, shared with Kavya by end of Week 4.

Common mistakes

❌ Vague concepts ("a free finance guide")
❌ Things only Sir can write — we want lead magnets that don't need Sir's time
❌ Things that take 2 weeks to produce — keep them producible in 1-2 days

Every day · target 95% response within 4 hours

What this means

Every working day, open Sir's IG and LinkedIn. Reply to comments and DMs from the last 24 hours. Goal: within 4 hours of someone reaching out.

Why we do it

When someone takes time to comment or DM Sir and we reply, it builds loyalty. When we ignore them, it kills loyalty. Engagement is the daily heartbeat.

Common mistakes

❌ Replying in YOUR voice, not Sir's
❌ Letting DMs pile up over the weekend
❌ Sales-pitching in comments (looks desperate)

Task 5: Ship 4 LinkedIn posts + 5 IG posts for Sir

Across Weeks 1-3 · Riya reviews drafts, Kavya approves before publish

What this means

Write Sir's posts in his voice. Schedule them. Riya reviews drafts. Kavya approves before they go live.

Why we do it

Sir's daily content is what keeps SSEI's brand alive on social. Without it, audience growth stops.

How to do LinkedIn (longer-form, professional)

  1. Read sir_voice.md for tone.
  2. Pick a topic — something Sir cares about (finance education, why CFA matters, student stories, industry analysis).
  3. Write 150-250 words with this structure:
    • Hook — first 1-2 lines that make people stop scrolling
    • Body — 3-5 short paragraphs (NOT walls of text)
    • Takeaway — 1 line that summarizes the lesson
  4. Share draft with Riya in Slack DM. She reviews.
  5. After her edits, share with Kavya for final approval.
  6. Schedule it.

How to do IG (visual + caption)

  1. Same voice study.
  2. For each post, decide format: carousel? reel script? quote card? single image?
  3. Write the caption (60-150 words). Strong hook + 3-4 lines + CTA.
  4. Suggest the visual (don't design — just describe what the visual should show).
  5. Same review path: Riya → Kavya → schedule.

Targets

  • 1 LinkedIn post in Week 1
  • 3 LinkedIn posts in Week 2
  • 5 IG posts in Week 3

Example of a good Sir LinkedIn opening

"I tell my CFA students this every batch — your prep isn't broken. Your study schedule is."

First person. Specific audience. Hooks immediately.

Common mistakes

❌ Writing in YOUR voice instead of Sir's
❌ Corporate jargon ("synergies", "leverage", "ecosystem") — Sir doesn't talk like that
❌ No clear hook in first line
❌ Skipping Riya's review
❌ "ChatGPT-flavored" writing — Sir hates this

🚨 How to ask for help

If you're stuck for more than 15 minutes:

  1. Re-read the task in your dashboard.
  2. Re-read this playbook.
  3. Still stuck? WhatsApp Kavya at +91 75950 53300.

How to ask a good question

Don't ask: "How do I do this?" — too vague.

Do ask like this:

"Stuck on Task 1 — influencer research. I tried searching YouTube for 'CFA prep India' but most results are 5+ years old and the creators haven't posted in a year. The thing I don't understand is: should I still log them if their content is good but they've gone quiet?"
❌ "Hi, can you help me with task 1?"
❌ "I'm confused about the influencer thing"
❌ "What should I do next?"

Specific questions get specific answers in 2 minutes. Vague questions sit for hours.
